Notes

The Helms Degenerate Art Show was a large-scale protest exhibition organized by Ron English and Shawn Eichman in response to Senator Jesse Helms, NEA censorship battles, and the new U.S. flag-desecration law.

Framed in reference to the Nazi “Degenerate Art” exhibition, the project set out to present the kinds of works a censorious culture would try to suppress. More than 300 artists ultimately took part across studios, galleries, and alternative spaces in New York, with programming that included flag-based works, performances, video, poetry, and political action.

A later installment opened on December 9, 1989 at Black and White in Color Gallery in the South Bronx, featuring artists such as Ron English, Faith Ringgold, Leon Golub, Nancy Spero, Dread Scott, Richard Serra, Lady Pink, Christo, and Joe Coleman. Promotional materials also described open studios, a graffiti walk, and ongoing film, music, performance, and visual art events under the exhibition banner.
