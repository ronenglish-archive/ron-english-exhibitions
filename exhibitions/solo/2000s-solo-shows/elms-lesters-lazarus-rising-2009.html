Notes

Lazarus Rising was Ron English’s first UK solo exhibition with Elms Lesters Painting Rooms, opening on 8 May 2009 in London. The show presented monumental canvases and politically charged compositions, including large-scale reinterpretations of Picasso’s Guernica and related works that pushed English’s Popaganda language into historical and war-themed territory.

The exhibition positioned English’s practice as a dialogue between classic modernist painting and contemporary mass-media imagery. Satirical takes on propaganda, religion, and consumer culture appeared in dense, highly worked compositions that reinforced his reputation for merging pop iconography with sharp social critique.

The show was accompanied by a hardcover catalogue, also titled Lazarus Rising, which documented the paintings and provided added context for the series. Together, the exhibition and publication form an important record of English’s work in London at the end of the 2000s.
