Notes

The Secret History of KISS was Ron English’s 2008 solo exhibition at Shooting Gallery in San Francisco’s Larkin Street district, timed to coincide with the final stretch of the U.S. election season. The show brought together English’s high-saturation Popaganda imagery with political parody, celebrity critique, and his irreverent reworking of rock iconography.

The exhibition reframed the band KISS through English’s satirical visual language, blending branding tropes, comic distortion, and cultural commentary. Contemporary coverage noted strong attendance and broad local attention from gallery writers, art blogs, and event reviewers.

As a San Francisco presentation from late 2008, the show is a useful record of how English’s gallery work moved between pop spectacle, political imagery, and music-related cultural references during this period.
