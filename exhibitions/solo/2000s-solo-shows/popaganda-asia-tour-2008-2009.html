Notes

POPaganda Asia Tour brought Ron English’s work to audiences across Asia during 2008 and 2009 through a linked sequence of retail-gallery presentations, promotional events, and related media coverage. The tour launched in Hong Kong and extended to Manila and Shanghai, framing English’s work for collectors and pop-art audiences in a strongly international context.

Coverage from the period emphasized the broad appeal of English’s Popaganda imagery, including political references, altered cultural icons, and collectible formats associated with designer toy culture and crossover exhibition spaces. The Manila stop also connected to the circulation of his Abraham Obama imagery during the early months of 2009.

As a touring series rather than a single fixed exhibition, POPaganda Asia Tour is an important record of English’s overseas visibility at the end of the decade and of how his work moved between gallery presentation, pop-culture retail environments, and international promotional channels.
