Notes

This 2009 exhibition at The Don Gallery in Milan marked Ron English’s first solo show in Italy. Running from 9 July to 25 August 2009, it presented a focused selection of new paintings and sculptures shaped by political satire, pop iconography, and English’s Popaganda sensibility.

Coverage around the show stressed how English’s imagery translated into the Italian contemporary art context. Cartoon mascots, corporate logos, and broader cultural symbols appeared beside more direct political work, introducing Milan audiences to his critique of advertising, consumerism, and media imagery.

The exhibition also stands as an early record of The Don Gallery’s support for international Pop Surrealism and related lowbrow-inflected painting, making it a useful entry in English’s European exhibition history.
