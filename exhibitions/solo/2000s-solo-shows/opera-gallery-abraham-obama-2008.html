Notes

On October 2, 2008, Opera Gallery in New York hosted an event centered on Ron English’s Abraham Obama painting, a composite portrait merging the features of Abraham Lincoln and Barack Obama. The original canvas was offered alongside a group of hand-finished colorway variants.

The release landed during the final stretch of the 2008 U.S. presidential election and connected English’s Popaganda practice to one of the most visible political moments of the decade. The image moved beyond the gallery setting through reproductions, press attention, and later circulation online and in print.

As a gallery presentation, Abraham Obama marks an important point where English’s political iconography, pop portraiture, and street-art sensibility converged in a single work that reached a broad public audience.
