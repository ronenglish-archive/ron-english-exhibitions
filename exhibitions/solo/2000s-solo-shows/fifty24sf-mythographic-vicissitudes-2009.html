Notes

Mythographic Vicissitudes opened on January 8, 2009, at San Francisco’s FIFTY24SF Gallery, presenting a focused body of new figurative works by Ron English. The exhibition expanded his ongoing exploration of mythic symbolism, pop-culture distortion, and hyperreal consumer archetypes.

Preview coverage framed the show through English’s hybrid allegorical language, bringing together reimagined cartoon figures, Americana, religious references, and saturated pseudo-mythological imagery. Opening-night coverage also noted its pairing with Alex Pardee’s concurrent exhibition, placing it within a busy early-2009 program at Upper Playground’s flagship gallery space.

The exhibition is a useful record of English’s West Coast gallery presence at the close of the decade and of how his Popaganda imagery continued to shift toward mythic and symbolic frameworks while keeping its sharp engagement with popular culture.
