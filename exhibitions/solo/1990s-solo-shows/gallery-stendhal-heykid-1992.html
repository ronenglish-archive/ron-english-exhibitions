Notes

Ron English presented HEY! My Kid Could Do That! at Gallery Stendhal in late 1992. The exhibition title framed the show through a familiar dismissive phrase, then turned it into a pointed exploration of authorship, originality, and the visual language of childhood.

Contemporary coverage describes the exhibition as featuring works derived from children’s drawings and family collaborations, with images then reworked by English. A later magazine reference characterizes the show as paintings and drawings created by preschool children and “remastered” by the artist, underscoring the exhibition’s challenge to assumptions about skill, ownership, and value.

The documented materials also note that the show supported the Learning Through Art / Guggenheim Children’s Program, linking the exhibition’s theme to a broader educational and institutional context.
