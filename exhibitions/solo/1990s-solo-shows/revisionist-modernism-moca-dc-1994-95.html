Notes

Ron English presented Revisionist Modernism: New Works in Washington, D.C., in late 1994 and early 1995. Contemporary coverage describes the exhibition as a body of work built through direct re-engagement with canonical modern and popular art. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Reviews from the period note works that referenced Manet, Jasper Johns, Warhol, Frida Kahlo, Picasso, Rothko, Miró, and Norman Rockwell, among others. Rather than parodying those sources from a distance, English used them as material for transformation, updating familiar compositions and icons through his own pop-cultural language. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

The exhibition helps document a key mid-1990s stage in English’s development, when appropriation, revision, and visual collision became central to his gallery practice. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
