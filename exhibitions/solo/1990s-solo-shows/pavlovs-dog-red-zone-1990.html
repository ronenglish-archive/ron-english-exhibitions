Notes

This nightclub exhibition of Pavlov’s Dog: The Last Supper and Other Paintings took place at Red Zone in New York City in 1990. The invitation card places the event within the club’s regular nightlife programming rather than a conventional gallery setting. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The printed materials note the opening on Friday, May 25, hosted by Fred Rothbell-Mista, and include both a dress-code note and a five-dollar admission option. Those details help situate the exhibition within the downtown club scene that shaped part of Ron English’s public presentation during the period. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

By presenting the Pavlov’s Dog imagery alongside other paintings in this setting, the event linked English’s work to a nightlife audience and to alternative spaces where art, music, fashion, and spectacle overlapped. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
