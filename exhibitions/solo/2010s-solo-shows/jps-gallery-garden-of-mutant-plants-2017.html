Notes

Garden of Mutant Plants transformed JPS Gallery into a fantastical environment of mutant vegetation, hybrid forms, and immersive POPaganda imagery. Rather than treating the gallery as a neutral display space, the exhibition turned it into a themed installation built around Ron English’s altered natural world.

Presented in Hong Kong in spring 2017, the show extended English’s character-driven and satirical visual language into botanical fantasy, linking sculpture, environment, and theatrical display. The setting at Repulse Bay gave the project a distinct atmosphere, closer to an invented habitat than a standard wall-based exhibition.

As a 2017 international presentation, the exhibition is a useful record of how English’s practice could expand beyond paintings and editions into immersive installation grounded in world-building and symbolic mutation.
