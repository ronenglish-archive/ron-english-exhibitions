Notes

Sugar High focused on overconsumption, marketing imagery, and candy-coated mascots, presenting hybrid figures and sculptural interventions shaped around Ron English’s critique of global eating habits and childhood-targeted advertising.

Staged at Dorothy Circus Gallery in Rome, the exhibition pushed English’s POPaganda language toward food culture, branding, and the seductive visual logic of processed consumption. Familiar commercial forms appeared reworked into images that felt playful at first glance and more unsettling on closer view.

The surviving installation images make the exhibition useful for documenting the breadth of works shown and the way the gallery framed the project through a bright, immersive presentation.
