Notes

POPAGANDASTAN – New Works from Ron English was a large solo exhibition at Corey Helford Gallery in Los Angeles, expanding English’s satirical universe of brand icons, cultural mascots, and myth-inflected figures.

The show pushed the POPaganda aesthetic through new paintings and character-driven imagery, bringing commercial parody, political critique, and symbolic pop language together in a major West Coast presentation.

The surviving installation views make the exhibition useful for documenting both the scale of the gallery presentation and the density of English’s visual world during this period.
