Notes

Ron English / Guernica was a solo exhibition at Allouche Gallery’s Meatpacking District space in 2016, featuring eighteen new paintings that reworked Picasso’s Guernica through Ron English’s own visual language.

The exhibition positioned the historic anti-war masterpiece as a framework for reflecting on contemporary political cycles, media spectacle, violence, and reconstruction in an era shaped by constant image circulation.

The surviving installation views are especially useful for documenting the density and scale of the presentation, as well as the way English translated a canonical twentieth-century painting into a new body of contemporary works.
