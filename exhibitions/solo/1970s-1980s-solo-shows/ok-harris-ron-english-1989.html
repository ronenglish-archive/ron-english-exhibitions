Notes

This solo exhibition at Ivan Karp’s OK Harris Works of Art presented Ron English’s early photographic pieces and illusionistic staged scenes. The show extended his investigation into constructed reality through tableaux built in the studio and photographed as direct images rather than darkroom manipulations.

The exhibition placed these works in dialogue with the gallery’s reputation for conceptual and photo-based work. It also helps document an early moment when English’s interest in illusion, staged perception, and media language entered a more formal gallery context in New York.

As one of his first documented New York solo exhibitions, the show marks an important step in the development of ideas that would later surface in billboard interventions, POPaganda imagery, and large-scale painting.
