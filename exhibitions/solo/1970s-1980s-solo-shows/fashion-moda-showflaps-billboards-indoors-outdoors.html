Notes

This 1986 exhibition at The Salon, produced by Stefan Eins and hosted by Fashion Moda, foregrounded Ron English’s engagement with billboard language, street intervention, and the movement of public imagery into an interior exhibition setting.

The title itself links showflaps, billboards, and the contrast between indoors and outdoors, pointing to the way English was already working across public space, gallery display, and advertising culture during this period.

Presented at Fashion Moda in the Bronx, the exhibition belongs to an important early phase in English’s career, when his work was developing through direct confrontation with urban visual systems and institutional boundaries.
