Notes

Brand Royalty premiered around twenty new paintings in which Ron English riffed on luxury branding, mascots, and consumer icons. The exhibition extended his long-running POPaganda critique into a body of work focused on status, desire, and the symbolic power of brand identity.

Working through densely layered compositions and high-gloss painterly surfaces, English pushed familiar characters and commercial imagery toward sharper reflections on aspiration, hierarchy, and consumer spectacle.

As a 2021 exhibition at Allouche Gallery, the show is a useful record of how English continued to evolve his critique of consumer culture through polished large-scale paintings and a concentrated thematic presentation.
